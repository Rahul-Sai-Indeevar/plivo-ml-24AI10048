2,000 Step LLM Speedrun Summary

Architecture & Parameter Optimization

I built an optimized 4-Layer GPT architecture with a hidden dimension of 192 and 6 heads. To comfortably adhere to the 2,000,000 parameter cap while expanding the context window to 320, I enabled weight tying (tying the input embedding weights to the output projection weights) and stripped linear biases. This resulted in a highly expressive model taking up exactly 1,989,312 parameters (99.4% of the budget).

The Tokenizer Strategy

The biggest roadblock to lowering the BPB score was the Devanagari text. Standard byte-level tokenizers evaluate Hindi characters as 3 separate tokens, destroying context windows. I engineered a Python-based Character-Level Tokenizer that assigns single vocabulary IDs to multi-byte characters dynamically parsed from the corpus, while maintaining a strict 0-255 raw byte fallback mechanism. This is fully lossless while shrinking sequence lengths immensely.

Training Enhancements

Because we only had 2000 steps, I set dropout to 0.0 to prevent information loss during the short training window. I replaced Adam with AdamW, implemented Gradient Clipping at 1.0, and utilized a Cosine Annealing Learning Rate Scheduler with a 10% warmup phase to maximize convergence.

Human vs. Machine Contribution

Machine Done: AI generated the PyTorch boilerplate for the Cosine Scheduler, calculated the precise math for the tied weights, and helped script the raw byte-fallback dictionary logic in the tokenizer.
Human Done: The strategic insight to transition away from a raw byte tokenizer to combat the Devanagari 3-byte expansion, the specific hyperparameter tuning (Block 320, Batch 24, LR 8e-4, Zero Dropout), and calculating the precise parameter limits to maximize the 2M cap.

Next-Step Scaling Hypothesis (Compute Constraint Limit)

Given more training time, the theoretical limit of this challenge is a sub-1.50 BPB. This can be achieved by moving from a Character-Level Tokenizer to a 6,000-vocabulary Word-Level Tokenizer. Because BPB scales with token-to-byte compression, predicting whole words drastically lowers the total negative log-likelihood. To support this massive vocabulary under the 2,000,000 parameter cap, I designed a rebalanced architecture (Hidden Dim: 144, Layers: 4, Block Size: 256) resulting in exactly 1,897,488 parameters. This represents the ultimate theoretical optimization for this dataset.
